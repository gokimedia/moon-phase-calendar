Understanding the 8 Moon Phases

The moon completes a full cycle approximately every 29.53 days, passing through eight distinct phases. Each phase carries its own energetic quality, making the lunar cycle a powerful framework for setting intentions, reflecting, and releasing.

🌑

New Moon

Fresh starts, setting intentions, planting seeds for new projects.

🌒

Waxing Crescent

Building momentum, taking first steps, clarifying goals.

🌓

First Quarter

Decision-making, overcoming challenges, commitment.

🌔

Waxing Gibbous

Refining, adjusting, patience as things develop.

🌕

Full Moon

Culmination, celebration, heightened intuition and clarity.

🌖

Waning Gibbous

Gratitude, sharing knowledge, introspection.

🌗

Last Quarter

Releasing, letting go, forgiveness, breaking habits.

🌘

Waning Crescent

Rest, surrender, reflection before the next cycle begins.

Moon Phases and Tarot

Many tarot readers align their practice with the lunar cycle for deeper, more resonant readings. The Moon card (Major Arcana XVIII) directly represents intuition, illusion, and the subconscious — themes that are amplified during certain lunar phases.

Best Practices by Phase

New Moon: Ideal for intention-setting spreads. Ask the cards what seeds to plant this cycle. Pull a single card for your monthly theme.

Full Moon: Perfect for deep, complex spreads like the Celtic Cross. The veil between conscious and subconscious is thinnest. Full moon readings tend to be the most vivid and revealing.

Waning Moon: Focus on release and shadow work. Ask the cards what you need to let go of, what patterns no longer serve you.

Lunar Gardening and Rituals

Beyond spiritual practice, moon phases have been used for centuries to guide agricultural planting, hair cutting, and personal rituals. The gravitational pull of the moon affects tides, and many traditional cultures believe it influences plant growth and human energy levels.

Whether you follow the moon for spiritual alignment, agricultural timing, or simply enjoy tracking the night sky, understanding the lunar cycle connects you to one of humanity's oldest rhythms.
